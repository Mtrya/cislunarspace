# SpaceX GPS III SV10 Launch Preview: Tenth GPS III Satellite Ready for Liftoff

**Summary:** SpaceX is targeting April 21, 2026 at 06:53 UTC (14:53 Beijing time) to launch GPS III SV10, the tenth satellite in the U.S. GPS III constellation upgrade program, aboard a Falcon 9 Block 5 from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, Florida. GPS III SV10 will further enhance the accuracy, signal strength, and anti-jamming capabilities of the U.S. Global Positioning System.

![Falcon 9 rocket awaiting launch (file photo)](./figures/2026-04-21-gps-iii-sv10/hero.jpg)

*Credit: SpaceX / TheSpaceDevs*

## Mission Overview

GPS III SV10 is the tenth satellite in the GPS III constellation upgrade program built by Lockheed Martin, and the second-to-last GPS III satellite in the planned 11-satellite constellation. The GPS III satellites offer significant improvements over the previous GPS IIF generation:

- **Improved Accuracy:** Civil signal accuracy up to three times better than previous generation
- **Stronger Signals:** Enhanced L1, L2, and L5 signals with improved anti-jamming capabilities
- **Extended Lifespan:** 15-year design life (compared to 12 years for previous generations)
- **L5 Signal:** New aviation-safe L5 signal frequency for improved aviation navigation

This launch will use a flight-proven Falcon 9 first stage booster. The launch window spans from 06:53 to 07:08 UTC (15 minutes), with a launch probability of 90%. Weather concerns include Cumulus Cloud Rule and Thick Cloud Layers Rule.

## GPS III Program Background

GPS III is the U.S. Air Force's third-generation GPS satellite replacement program, designed to replace aging GPS IIR and GPS IIF satellites. Once all 11 GPS III satellites are deployed, they will operate alongside existing GPS IIF and GPS III SV01–SV09 satellites to provide Position, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) services to users worldwide. GPS III satellites are primarily manufactured by Lockheed Martin, with Boeing and Northrop Grumman contributing to various subsystems.
