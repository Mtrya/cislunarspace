# Long March 10B Successfully Completes Maiden Flight, Validates World's First Sea-Based Net Recovery Technology

**Summary:** On April 28, 2026, the Long March 10B (CZ-10B) carrier rocket successfully executed its maiden flight from the Wenchang Commercial Spaceport in Hainan, China. The mission not only achieved a successful maiden flight but also simultaneously validated the world's first sea-based net recovery technology, marking a major breakthrough for China's commercial space sector in large-capacity reusable rockets.

![Long March 10B carrier rocket maiden flight (Sina News image)](./figures/2026-04-28-changzheng-10yi-maiden-flight/hero.jpg)
*Image source: Sina News*

## Mission Overview

The Long March 10B rocket was developed by the China Aerospace Science and Technology Corporation (CASC). This maiden flight mission lifted off from Launch Complex 2 at the Wenchang Commercial Spaceport in Hainan. The rocket performed nominally, and the payload was successfully delivered to its intended orbit, marking a complete success for the inaugural flight.

Compared to SpaceX's Falcon 9, which had previously demonstrated orbital-class reusability, the Long March 10B adopts a booster-less configuration with a 5-meter diameter body and seven YF-100N liquid oxygen-kerosene engines on its first stage. Its simplified design and lower maintenance costs make it more suitable for commercial operations.

## World's First: Sea-Based Net Recovery Technology

The core highlight of this mission was the validation of the world's first sea-based net recovery technology (colloquially known as "catching the rabbit"). Unlike traditional land-based precise landing recovery, the Long March 10B's first stage, upon return, uses grid fins for attitude control. After engine deceleration, it is captured by a flexible net deployed in the landing sea area.

Key advantages of this technology include:

- **Lower landing precision requirements**: Less affected by sea waves, can be captured within a larger area
- **Lower launch risks**: Avoids damage to people or structures from failed land landings
- **Higher turnaround efficiency**: Reports indicate the first stage can be inspected and re-launched within 72 hours
- **More reuse cycles**: Designed for over 10 reuse cycles

The successful validation of this system makes China the second country after the United States to master reusable rocket recovery technology for large-capacity launch vehicles, laying the foundation for reducing China's commercial launch costs by 30% to 50%.

## Payload Capacity and Market Prospects

The Long March 10B's payload capacity in reusable mode can reach over 16 tons to Low Earth Orbit (LEO), surpassing the early Falcon 9's 10-ton-class performance. This rocket will serve as the primary launch vehicle for China's Qianfan Constellation, a low-earth-orbit broadband internet satellite constellation.

The Qianfan Constellation is China's version of Starlink, planned to deploy over 13,000 low-orbit broadband satellites, with Phase 1 targeting 608 satellites by 2025-2027. The Long March 10B's high capacity and low cost will significantly accelerate the construction of this national project.

## Industry Impact

The successful maiden flight of the Long March 10B holds landmark significance for China's commercial space industry chain. Aerospace Engineering (603698) provides separation and reset systems along with gas power generation systems, Juligroup (002342) develops key recovery net components, Guanglian Aviation (300900) participates in structural component manufacturing, Sirui Materials (688102) supplies copper alloy materials, Aerospace Electronics (600879) provides telemetry systems, and Jiufeng Energy (605090) offers propellant transportation services.

As reusable rocket technology matures, China's commercial launch costs are expected to drop from the current ~$20,000/kg to below $10,000/kg, substantially enhancing international competitiveness.
