## Definition

**Changeite-Mg (Magnesium Changeite)** is one of the **two new lunar minerals** discovered from Chang'e-5 lunar sample research, announced by the China National Space Administration (CNSA) on April 24, 2026 (the other being Changeite-Ce). As the **third new mineral** identified from Chang'e-5 samples, it enriches the lunar mineral database and provides new research objects for planetary mineralogy.

## Crystal Characteristics

Changeite-Mg belongs to the **calcium rare-earth phosphate mineral** family, sharing the same mineral category with previously discovered Changeite. However, Changeite-Mg is notably **richer in magnesium and rare earth elements**. Key features:

- **Crystal habit**: **Columnar crystals**
- **Grain size**: Only **2 to 30 micrometers**, approximately 1/30 to 1/3 of human hair diameter
- **Occurrence**: Primarily found in **lunar basaltic rock fragments** from drilling samples

## Scientific Significance

The discovery of Changeite-Mg holds the following scientific value:

1. **Enriches the lunar mineral database**: As the third new mineral discovered from lunar samples (following Changeite and Changeite-Ce), it provides entirely new research subjects for lunar studies
2. **Reveals lunar evolution information**: Changeite-Mg records different stages of lunar evolution, helping scientists better understand the Moon's formation and evolutionary history
3. **Advances planetary mineralogy**: This discovery propels China's planetary mineralogy research to a deeper level
4. **Resource development foundation**: Researchers will continue studying new material compositions in lunar regolith samples to provide scientific basis for future lunar resource exploration and utilization
