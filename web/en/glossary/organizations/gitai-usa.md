## Company Overview

GITAI is a Japanese space robotics startup founded in Tokyo in 2016, with a U.S. subsidiary, GITAI USA Inc., headquartered in California. GITAI's mission is to dramatically reduce the labor costs of space operations through space robotics technology, making on-orbit servicing and lunar development economically viable. The company has received support from the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) and multiple U.S. government agencies, and has been selected as a supplier in the MDA SHIELD IDIQ vendor pool.

## Space and Cislunar-Related Business

### Space Robotic Arms and Robots

GITAI's core products are space-specialized robotic arms and mobile robots:

- **GITAI Robotic Arm**: A dual robotic arm system designed specifically for space on-orbit servicing, featuring 6-DOF redundant robotic arms capable of performing tasks such as target grasping, spacecraft inspection, and module replacement. The robotic arm is equipped with haptic feedback and vision-guided systems, supporting both remote operation and autonomous modes.
- **GITAI Rover**: A lunar surface mobile platform capable of carrying scientific instruments and operational tools, supporting autonomous navigation and remote control.

### Lunar Lander

GITAI has a contract with JAXA to provide robotic arm systems for the Japanese crewed lunar rover:

- **JAXA Crewed Lunar Rover Robotic Arm**: As part of the JAXA-led crewed lunar exploration mission, GITAI is developing operational robotic arms installed on the lunar rover for geological sampling, equipment deployment, and lunar surface facility maintenance.

### On-Orbit Servicing and Space Debris Handling

GITAI is a member of the MDA SHIELD IDIQ (a vendor pool led by Canadian company MDA for space services):

- **On-Orbit Servicing (OOS)**: GITAI participates in bids for space debris removal and spacecraft on-orbit repair projects; its robotic arm technology is core to executing such missions.
- **Active Debris Removal**: The company is developing specialized debris grasping mechanical systems and orbital transfer platforms.

## Connection to Cislunar Space Missions

GITAI is one of the most directly cislunar-related commercial companies:

- **Lunar Surface Operations**: The JAXA contract means GITAI's robotic arms will be present within human activity areas in the lunar south pole region.
- **On-Orbit Service Expansion**: As cislunar space accumulates a growing number of defunct satellites and debris in the future, GITAI's on-orbit servicing capabilities can execute value satellite maintenance or debris removal missions in this region.
- **Cost Reduction**: Large-scale application of space robotics can reduce the cost of individual on-orbit operations by one to two orders of magnitude, making it a key enabling technology for cislunar economic development.
